/ 06 — questions, answered

Do I have to sign a contract?

No. Monthly plans are month-to-month — if it isn't working for you, stop with a week's notice. One-time builds use a simple one-page agreement so we both know exactly what's being made.

Who owns the website when it's done?

You do. The domain, the code, the content, the accounts, and your customer list are yours from day one. I never hold a site hostage.

I already have a website. Can you still help?

Yes — the growth plan works with whatever you already have. And if your site needs a refresh, I'll give you an honest read on whether it's worth updating or rebuilding.

How soon will a growth plan make a difference?

The honest answer: Google Business work tends to show in the first month or two, while local search visibility compounds over three to six. Every month you get a recap so you can judge for yourself.

Do you only work with Bowling Green businesses?

Bowling Green and the surrounding area is home base, and local clients get in-person time. But websites and apps travel fine — I take remote projects too.

Why work with a one-person studio instead of an agency?

You talk to the person doing the work — the same one who designed, built, and shipped a live App Store app. No account managers, no handoffs, and no agency overhead in the middle.
